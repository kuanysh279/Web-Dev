Basic info

Horses (Equus ferus caballus) are social herd animals with a lifespan of 25–30 years. They stand 14–17 hands tall, weigh 900–2,200 pounds, and eat grass, hay, grains, and water. Horses move with four gaits: walk, trot, canter, and gallop. They are categorized as draft horses (strong), light horses (agile), ponies (small), and wild types.

types of this animal

Draft Horses: Large, strong horses like Clydesdales and Percherons, used for pulling heavy loads.
Light Horses: Agile and fast breeds like Arabians and Thoroughbreds, ideal for riding and racing.
Ponies: Small, sturdy horses under 14.2 hands, such as Shetland ponies.
Wild Horses: Free-roaming breeds like Mustangs, living in the wild.
Gaited Horses: Breeds like Tennessee Walkers with naturally smooth, unique gaits for comfortable riding.
Warmbloods: A mix of draft and light horse traits, used in sports like dressage and jumping.
